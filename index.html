A diet rich in vegetables can lower blood pressure, reduce the risk of heart disease and stroke prevent some types of cancer, lower risk of eye and digestive problems, and have a positive effect upon blood sugar, which can help keep appetite in check.

Milk and milk products contribute many nutrients, such as calcium, vitamin D (for products fortified with vitamin D), and potassium, to the diet. Consuming milk and milk products is linked to improved bone health, especially in children and adolescents.
